LaySumm

Summaries / abstracts of technical documents and research articles are written by specialists for specialists. They are often quite inaccessible to non-expert (lay) readers. Summarization systems that can automatically produce lay summaries of technical documents can help remove the apparent mysteriousness of science. Lay summarization requires some rephrasing (such as the recognition and re-phrasing of in-domain terminology and expressions) and some original content insertion (such as everyday analogical examples). In this task we evaluate systems' ability to perform the former and indicate the presence and location (but not the actual content) of the latter. Training and test data will be full-length scientific articles, their scientific abstracts, and their lay summaries (all created by the authors). The evaluation metric will be ROUGE.
